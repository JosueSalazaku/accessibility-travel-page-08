FOODIE ON THE ROAD
TRAVELS FOODS

Chao Phrom Market's Pad Thai Haven

At Ayutthaya's Chao Phrom Market, a small stall served up Pad Thai perfection. The blend of noodles, prawns, and zesty flavors was a taste of Thai street food mastery.


Village Delights at Kalandula

In a quaint village near Kalandula Falls, I savored authentic Angolan cuisine. The Moamba de Galinha, a fragrant chicken stew, complemented cassava porridge flawlessly. The warm hospitality and flavors left an indelible mark.


Neptune Oyster's Lobster Roll

In Boston's North End, Neptune Oyster redefines the Lobster Roll. Succulent lobster, buttery brioche, and crispy fries create a seafood masterpiece.


Andean Delights at Tinkuy

At Tinkuy in Machu Picchu, try the Cuy al Horno (roasted guinea pig) for an authentic Peruvian taste. Served with native potatoes and aji sauce, it's a must-try Andean delight with a view of the ruins.


Mangalemi's Fergese:

In Berat's historic town, Mangalemi's Fergese is a creamy blend of peppers, tomatoes, and local cheese. The smoky, savory, and tangy flavors offer a taste of Albania's culinary heritage.


Authentic Flavors at Dolmama

Dolmama in Yerevan is an Armenian culinary gem. Their Dolma, with tender grape leaves and aromatic rice, offered a burst of traditional flavors. A must-visit for any food enthusiast in Yerevan.


Seafood Splendor at Izumiya

In Tsushima, Izumiya dazzled with its Sashimi Moriawase. The freshest catch of the day was a visual and gustatory masterpiece. Each slice showcased Tsushima's coastal bounty.


Cheese Haven: Queijaria Ovelha Negra
