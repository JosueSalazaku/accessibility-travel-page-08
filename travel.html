DESTINATION HIGHLIGHTS & TRAVEL TIPS


Ayutthaya, Thailand
Historical Marvels and Culinary Delights
Nestled along the Chao Phraya River, Ayutthaya is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an enchanting window into Thailand's rich history. Explore ancient temples like Wat Mahathat, where tree roots intertwine with stone relics. For an optimal experience, consider hiring a local guide who can provide insightful historical context. After a day of exploration, dive into Ayutthaya's renowned culinary scene. Opt for street food stalls with long queues â€“ a sure sign of quality. Don't forget to try local specialties like boat noodles, succulent river prawns, and the exquisite Khanom Krok.


Berat, Albania
The City of a Thousand Windows
Berat, often called the "City of a Thousand Windows," is a picturesque town nestled in the heart of Albania. Its UNESCO-listed old town, known as Mangalem, boasts Ottoman-era architecture and stunning views from the hilltop castle. To fully appreciate the charm, consider staying in a traditional guesthouse within the old town. Immerse yourself in Albania's unique cuisine with dishes like Fergese, a delectable blend of peppers, tomatoes, and cheese. Visit local markets for fresh produce and interact with friendly vendors.

Boston, USA
Historical Treasures and Culinary
Diversity Boston, one of America's oldest cities, seamlessly blends history with modernity. Stroll along the Freedom Trail, exploring landmarks like Paul Revere's House and the USS Constitution Museum. Consider purchasing a Boston CityPASS for discounted access to major attractions. When it comes to food, Boston offers a diverse culinary landscape. Savor succulent seafood at Quincy Market, indulge in chowder, and don't miss out on the city's famous lobster rolls. Keep in mind that New England weather can be unpredictable, so pack layers!


Kalandula Falls, Angola
Nature's Majestic Masterpiece
Hidden within Angola's lush landscape, Kalandula Falls is one of Africa's most spectacular waterfalls. To capture the best views, embark on a guided hike that leads to various vantage points. Don't forget comfortable footwear and a camera! Surrounding the falls is a vibrant array of flora and fauna, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts and photographers alike. Be sure to bring binoculars for birdwatching and insect repellent for a comfortable experience.
