Ayutthaya, Thailand
Historical Marvels and Culinary Delights Nestled along the Chao Phraya River, Ayutthaya is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an enchanting window into Thailand's rich history. Explore ancient temples like Wat Mahathat, where tree roots intertwine with stone relics. For an optimal experience, consider hiring a local guide who can provide insightful historical context. After a day of exploration, dive into Ayutthaya's renowned culinary scene. Opt for street food stalls with long queues â€“ a sure sign of quality. Don't forget to try local specialties like boat noodles, succulent river prawns, and the exquisite Khanom Krok.


Berat, Albania
The City of a Thousand Windows Berat, often called the "City of a Thousand Windows," is a picturesque town nestled in the heart of Albania. Its UNESCO-listed old town, known as Mangalem, boasts Ottoman-era architecture and stunning views from the hilltop castle. To fully appreciate the charm, consider staying in a traditional guesthouse within the old town. Immerse yourself in Albania's unique cuisine with dishes like Fergese, a delectable blend of peppers, tomatoes, and cheese. Visit local markets for fresh produce and interact with friendly vendors.
